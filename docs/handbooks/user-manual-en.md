## Who does what on Zook

Zook is the software a gym runs on — from the front desk to the workout plan on your phone. Everyone who touches it has one of six roles:

| Role | In one sentence | Where they work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Member** | Joins a gym, checks in, follows a plan, shops. | The Zook mobile app |
| **Owner** | Runs the gym as a business — pricing, staff, money, growth. | The web dashboard, plus a mobile owner view |
| **Admin** | Helps the owner run daily operations, with fewer money/billing permissions. | Same as Owner |
| **Receptionist** | Staffs the front desk — check-ins, payments, pickups. | The desk web app, or mobile |
| **Trainer** | Coaches members — plans, notes, progress. | Mobile trainer view, or the web coach view |
| **Platform operator** | A Zook employee who keeps the whole system healthy. | The internal platform console |

If you're not sure which one you are, look at what you see when you open the app: a scan button and a shop tab means you're a **Member**; a revenue chart means you're an **Owner** or **Admin**; a check-in queue means you're a **Receptionist**; a client list means you're a **Trainer**.

## Signing in

Everyone signs in the same way, regardless of role.

1. Open the Zook app (or a web page that asks you to sign in).
2. Enter your phone number or email address.
3. Zook sends you a one-time code (an **OTP**) by SMS or email. Type it in.
4. On iPhone or Android, you can also sign in with **Google** or **Apple** if that option is shown — it skips the OTP step entirely.

That's it — you don't need a password. If you don't receive the code within a minute, tap **Resend**. If it still doesn't arrive, check that you typed your phone/email correctly, and see [Getting help](#getting-help).

**Why no password?** Fewer things to forget, and it's harder for someone else to break into your account without your phone.

## Guide for Members

If you go to the gym and use the Zook app to check in, follow a workout plan, or buy things from the gym shop — this section is for you.

### Getting started in 5 steps

1. **Sign in** (see above).
2. **Find your gym** — tap **Find gyms** on the Home tab, or search by name or city.
3. **Open the gym's page** — you'll see its photos, plans, trainers, and location.
4. **Pick a membership plan** and pay (or, if the gym requires approval first, send a join request and wait to be approved).
5. **Check in** the next time you're at the gym, using the **Scan** tab.

### The five tabs at the bottom of the app

| Tab | What you'll find there |
| --- | --- |
| **Home** | Your membership status, how many days are left, and whatever you should do next (renew, complete today's workout, etc.) |
| **Scan** | Check in at the gym by scanning a QR code, or typing in a code if the camera isn't working |
| **Plans** | Your workout, diet, or habit plan, if your gym or trainer has assigned one |
| **Shop** | Products the gym sells — supplements, gear, merchandise — with pickup at the front desk |
| **Profile** | Your account details, privacy settings, language, and support |

### Joining a gym and paying

1. Open the gym's page and tap the plan you want.
2. If you have a **referral code** or a **coupon**, enter it now — the discount applies before you pay.
3. Check the final amount carefully. It should clearly show the plan price, any discount, and the total.
4. Pay using whichever method the gym offers (card, UPI, netbanking, or in some cases, cash at the desk).
5. If the plan renews automatically, you'll be offered **autopay** — turn it on if you don't want to worry about renewing manually every month.
6. Once payment succeeds, your membership becomes active immediately. You'll see this reflected on the Home tab.

**A few special cases:**
- **Gym requires approval:** instead of paying immediately, you send a join request. The gym's owner or receptionist reviews it, and you'll be notified once approved.
- **Invite-only gym:** you'll need an invite or referral code before you can even see the plans.
- **You're under 18:** a parent or guardian needs to give consent before your membership can start. The app will guide you through this.

**If a payment fails:** don't worry, you haven't been charged for something that didn't work. Try again, or try a different payment method. If money was deducted but your membership didn't activate, contact the gym — see [Getting help](#getting-help).

### Checking in at the gym

This is the thing you'll do most often, so it's built to be fast.

1. Open the **Scan** tab.
2. Point your camera at the QR code posted at the gym's entrance.
3. You'll immediately see one of three results:
   - ✅ **Approved** — you're checked in, walk right in.
   - ⏳ **Pending** — the desk needs to double-check something first (this is rare). Show your phone to the receptionist.
   - ❌ **Rejected** — something's wrong (membership expired, wrong gym, etc.). The app tells you why, and what to do about it.

**If the camera won't scan** (bad lighting, camera permission denied, or a slow connection), tap **Enter code manually** and type in the code shown at the entrance instead. It works exactly the same way.

### Following your plan

If your gym or trainer has set you up with a workout, diet, or habit plan:

1. Open the **Plans** tab.
2. You'll see today's exercises, habits, or meals, laid out one at a time.
3. As you complete something, tap to mark it done — your progress is saved automatically.
4. During a workout, if an exercise has a rest period, the app shows a countdown timer between sets so you know exactly when to start the next one.
5. You can also see your progress over time — how consistent you've been, and (if you're tracking things like weight) a simple chart showing the trend.

**Adding your own exercise:** if you did something extra that wasn't on the plan, you can add a custom exercise for the day. It's saved just for that day and shows up the next time you check your history.

### Using the shop

The gym might sell supplements, gear, or merchandise right from the app.

1. Open the **Shop** tab and browse or search for what you want.
2. Add items to your cart.
3. Check out and pay.
4. You'll get a **pickup code** — show this to the front desk when you go to collect your order. They'll scan or type it in to confirm the handover.

Nothing gets marked as "picked up" until the desk actually hands it to you, so there's no risk of being charged without receiving your order.

### Notifications and your profile

- **Notifications** tell you about things like an upcoming renewal, a new message from your trainer, or a shop order that's ready for pickup.
- **Profile** is where you manage your account: update your phone/email, change the app's language, switch between light and dark appearance, and reach support if you need help.
- **Your data is yours.** From Profile, you can request an export of your data, or ask for your account to be deleted, at any time.

### Frequently asked questions (Members)

**Q: I switched phones. How do I get back into my account?**
A: Just sign in again with the same phone number or email — your membership and history are tied to your account, not your device.

**Q: Can I belong to more than one gym?**
A: Yes. Once you've joined multiple gyms, you can switch between them from your Profile.

**Q: What happens if my membership expires while I'm mid-workout?**
A: Nothing changes mid-session — but you won't be able to check in again until you renew.

**Q: I don't want to share my phone number with the gym.**
A: The gym only sees what it needs to run your membership (name, contact info for renewal reminders, attendance). You control the rest from your privacy settings.

**Q: My referral code isn't working.**
A: Referral codes usually need to be entered *before* you pay, not after. Double-check for typos, and make sure the code hasn't expired.

## Guide for Gym Owners

If you own or run a gym on Zook, this is your day-to-day operating guide.

### What you're in charge of

As the owner, you control everything about how your gym runs on Zook: your gym's public profile, membership plans and pricing, staff and their permissions, payments and refunds, the shop, reports, and your own billing with Zook.

### Setting up your gym for the first time

Do these once, in order, before you open your doors to members:

1. **Sign in** and open your dashboard.
2. **Fill in your gym profile** — name, logo, cover photo, address, and a short description. This is the first thing prospective members see.
3. **Add your branches** — if you have more than one location, set each one up with its own address and city.
4. **Create your membership plans** — give each one a clear name, price, and duration (e.g., "Monthly", "₹1,500", "1 month"). Members choose from exactly what you set up here.
5. **Add your staff** — invite receptionists, trainers, and admins, and assign each person the right role so they only see what they need to.
6. **Double-check your payment setup** — make sure the renewal flow works end-to-end before real members start paying.
7. **Preview your public gym page** — open it on both a phone and a desktop, and make sure it looks the way you want.

### Your daily routine

Most owners check in on Zook once or twice a day. Here's what to look at:

1. Open your **Today** view — it's a summary of everything that needs your attention right now.
2. **Review pending approvals** — new members waiting to join, if your gym requires approval.
3. **Check revenue and payments** — see what's come in, and whether any payments failed.
4. **Look at renewals coming due** — so you can nudge members before they lapse.
5. **Check attendance** — anything unusual, like a member who hasn't shown up in a while.
6. **Check your shop** — stock levels, and any orders that are ready for pickup.
7. Send members a notification only when it's genuinely useful — a reminder, an announcement, an offer. Nobody likes noise.

### Money and memberships — a few important habits

- **Keep the path to paying short.** Members should see a plan, its price, and a "Pay" button — not a maze of screens.
- **Name your plans clearly.** "Monthly", "Quarterly", "Annual — 2 months free" reads better than "Plan A", "Plan B".
- **Offer autopay.** It reduces the number of members who accidentally let their membership lapse — good for them, good for your revenue.
- **Refunds and cancellations should follow a policy you've decided in advance,** so every staff member handles them the same way.

### Your team

You can invite receptionists, trainers, and admins from your dashboard's Staff section. Each role sees a different set of tools:

- **Admins** can do almost everything you can, except a few sensitive money and billing actions.
- **Receptionists** handle check-ins, payments, and shop pickups at the front desk.
- **Trainers** manage their assigned members' plans and progress.

Every sensitive action anyone takes — a refund, a plan change, a staff permission change — is recorded in your **audit log**, so you always have a record of who did what.

### Reports and growth

Your dashboard's Reports section shows revenue, attendance, and member growth over time, so you can see whether your gym is trending in the right direction. You can also manage **coupons**, **offers**, and **referral rewards** to bring in new members.

### Frequently asked questions (Owners)

**Q: A member says they paid but their membership isn't active.**
A: Check the payment's status in your Payments section. If it succeeded on your end but the app didn't reflect it, this is worth escalating — see [Getting help](#getting-help).

**Q: Can I change a plan's price after members have already joined it?**
A: Yes, but it typically only affects new sign-ups and renewals going forward, not members who are mid-cycle on the old price.

**Q: How do I remove a staff member's access?**
A: From the Staff section of your dashboard, you can deactivate anyone's access instantly.

**Q: What's the difference between an Owner and an Admin?**
A: You (the Owner) have full control, including billing and business-level settings. Admins can run daily operations but don't get access to a few sensitive money and account-level actions.

## Guide for Admins

Admins help owners run the gym day to day. You're a powerful role — but not the owner, so a few billing and business-control actions may be off-limits to you.

### Your daily routine

1. Sign in and confirm which gym/branch you're working on.
2. Open the **Today** board for a quick summary.
3. Clear any pending member approvals.
4. Check attendance for anything unusual.
5. Review payments, shop orders, and stock levels.
6. Help any staff who run into issues.
7. If something sensitive happened, check the audit history to understand what changed.

### What you can do

| Area | What you can do |
| --- | --- |
| Members | Search, view details, approve or reject join requests |
| Plans | Create or update plans, if the owner has given you permission |
| Promotions | Manage coupons, offers, and referral rules |
| Attendance | Review anything unusual, manage QR check-in |
| Payments | Record an offline (cash/manual) payment, look up payment history |
| Shop | Manage products, stock levels, and orders |
| Staff | Invite or manage other staff, if permitted |
| Reports | Download reports for the owner to review |

### A few things to keep in mind

- Don't change real prices unless the owner has specifically asked you to.
- Don't issue a refund without the owner's approval, unless your gym's policy explicitly allows it.
- If you hit a "permission denied" message, that's expected for a few sensitive actions — ask the owner.
- Every sensitive thing you do shows up in the audit history, which is a good thing — it protects you too.

## Guide for Receptionists

You run the front desk. Your job is to be fast and accurate: let members in, look them up, take payments, and hand over shop orders.

### Your daily routine

1. Open your desk screen (web `/desk`, or the mobile reception view).
2. Confirm which branch you're working at.
3. Keep the member search and check-in screens ready to go — you'll use them constantly.
4. Verify each member's check-in as they arrive.
5. Only record a payment after you've double-checked the amount and the member.
6. Only mark a shop pickup as fulfilled after you've actually handed the item over.
7. If anything looks wrong or unclear, don't force it through — ask your owner or admin.

### Your main jobs, step by step

**Checking a member in:**
1. Scan or type in their attendance code.
2. Look at the result: approved, pending, or rejected.
3. Only let them in if it says approved. If it's pending or rejected, follow your gym's policy (usually: ask the owner/admin, or check what the rejection reason says).

**Looking up a member:**
1. Search by name, phone number, or their code.
2. Open their details to check their membership status before doing anything else (recording a payment, answering a question, etc.).

**Recording a payment:**
1. Select the member.
2. Enter the exact amount.
3. Choose the payment method (cash, card, etc.).
4. Confirm — and double-check the amount and member name before you do, since this can't easily be undone.

**Handing over a shop pickup:**
1. Open the ready order.
2. Ask the member for their pickup code.
3. Verify it matches.
4. Only then, mark it as fulfilled.

### A few important rules

- If the app says a member's entry is rejected or expired, don't let them in — even if they insist it's a mistake. Escalate instead.
- Never mark a pickup as fulfilled before the item is actually in the member's hands.
- Always double-check the payment amount and the member's name before confirming — it's much easier to catch a mistake before you tap Confirm than after.
- Avoid tapping Confirm or Fulfil more than once — if the app seems slow, wait rather than tapping again.

## Guide for Trainers

You coach members — assigning plans, tracking their progress, and staying in touch.

### Your daily routine

1. Open your trainer view on mobile.
2. Check which clients need your attention today.
3. Before a session, open that client's details and review their current plan and recent progress.
4. After the session, add notes, or update their plan based on how it went.
5. If you're using AI-drafted plans, always review them carefully before you publish — treat them as a first draft, not a final answer.
6. Confirm the member can actually see the plan you just published.

### What you'll find in the app

| Area | What it's for |
| --- | --- |
| Home | Today's summary and any clients who need attention |
| Clients | Search and open the clients assigned to you |
| Plans | Create, edit, assign, and publish workout/diet/habit plans |
| Notes | Save notes — either private (just for you) or visible to the member |
| Progress | See attendance, how consistently they're following their plan, and any progress entries they've logged |
| Inbox | Messages or check-ins from your members |

### A few important rules

- You'll only ever see clients who are assigned to you — this is by design, for everyone's privacy.
- If you use AI to help draft a plan, remember it's a suggestion, not a final instruction — read it fully before publishing.
- Be careful with anything touching medical conditions, injuries, diet, or supplements — only publish advice that's appropriate and matches your gym's policy.
- Your notes can shape a member's history with the gym, so keep them clear, factual, and respectful.

## Guide for Platform Operators

This section is for Zook's own internal team, keeping the whole system healthy across every gym on the platform.

### What you're responsible for

You monitor system health, provider status (payments, SMS, email), customer escalations, and anything unusual happening across the platform — not any single gym.

### Your main tools

| Area | What it's for |
| --- | --- |
| Platform console | A system-wide overview and operational controls |
| Status/readiness | Check that the API, database, and third-party providers are healthy |
| Webhooks | Inspect or, when safe, replay payment/provider events |
| Notifications | Review broadcast messages and delivery issues |
| Audit | Investigate any sensitive action taken anywhere on the platform |
| Support escalation | Help a specific gym, without bypassing policy |

### A few important rules

- Never impersonate a customer or modify their data without a documented support reason.
- Never share secrets — API keys, tokens, credentials — in screenshots, tickets, docs, or chat.
- Refunds, webhook replays, running production scripts, and mass notifications all need clear approval before you act.
- Keep a record of what you did: date, environment, action, and result.
- If a provider (like a payment gateway or SMS service) goes down, document the impact and a workaround before you touch any settings.

## Getting help

If something isn't working the way this guide says it should:

1. **Members and trainers:** open **Profile → Support** in the app.
2. **Owners, admins, and receptionists:** use the support option in your dashboard, or contact your Zook account contact.
3. **Everyone:** it helps a lot if you can say clearly — which gym and branch, which role you're signed in as, and exactly what you were trying to do when it went wrong.

If you're a staff member helping someone else troubleshoot, it's worth asking these questions in order:
1. Which gym and branch are they using?
2. Which role are they signed in as?
3. What were they trying to do?
4. Is this about a payment, attendance, membership, shop order, or plan?
5. Is this happening in the real app, or in a test/staging environment?
6. If the fix involves money, membership status, access, or customer data — get approval before acting.
7. Once resolved, note down what the final outcome was.

## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **OTP** | One-Time Password — a short code sent to your phone or email to sign in, instead of a password |
| **Check-in** | Scanning (or entering a code) to record your arrival at the gym |
| **Autopay** | Your membership renews and charges automatically each cycle, so you don't have to pay manually every time |
| **Pickup code** | A code you show at the front desk to collect a shop order you've already paid for |
| **Referral code** | A code you share (or receive) that gives a discount or reward when a friend joins using it |
| **Branch** | One physical location of a gym, if the gym has more than one |
| **Audit log** | A record of every sensitive action taken in the system, and who did it |
| **Plan** (membership) | A pricing package a member buys — e.g., "Monthly", "Annual" |
| **Plan** (workout) | A set of exercises, diet guidance, or habits assigned to a member by a trainer or the gym |
